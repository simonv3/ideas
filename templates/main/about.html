What is Idea Otter?

Do you have any idea? An inspiration? A possible project? Or maybe an answer to a question? A desire to collaborate?

If the answer was yes, then you're in the right place.

Idea Otter is a light and simple way to quickly store your ideas online. You can unleash them to the rest of the world by publishing them to the global field, or, if you like, you can keep a list of private ideas, so that you can look over them later, or see how your ideas change over time.

We believe in ideas. No matter how simple, ridiculous, or silly. Why? Because ideas lead to movement, which is what gets us there (you know the place I mean). That's why we set Idea Otter up. We want to enable you to share your ideas, to get feedback on them, and to discover more.

This isn't twitter, full of links, pancakes, and idleness. And it's not g+, full of good photos. This is an incubator, a place to store ideas until they're fully grown. (You can imagine ideas as baby otters, if you like. It doesn't help, but it sure is cute. That's pretty much the entire reason this is called IdeaOtter, actually.)

How does it work?

Whenever you think of something, just use the bookmarklet to jot it down. Other users of the site can than see what it is you thought of, comment on it, and help you expand your ideas. Use tags to make it indexable, if you can. We'll suggest some for you, too.

We don't want to get in the way of you and your thoughts, so we've made the process as simple as possible. Ideas can come at any moment, and you can't always act on them. What you think of could be the next Facebook, or save the planet. Maybe you don't have that idea right now - no bother - You can browse other users' ideas. If you like something, tell them. If you'd love to work on it, share it.

What are Slates?

Slates are a neat way of sharing ideas with friends or coworkers. Putting ideas out into the main stream can be a bit overwhelming - they can get drowned out easily. And what if you have a list of ideas that don't stand alone well, like thinking of good names for your pet otter?

That's where a slate comes in. You can create a slate, and post ideas to it - and they remain on that slate alone. That way, you can keep track of ideas separately from the main stream, and without using tags. You can invite your friends, either by email or through username, to join your slate, and work with you on it. When you're done, you can flush all of the slate into the main stream, too, if you like - any ideas marked as private will remain on the slate, but this way, if you have that killer otter name, you can share it with the world easily.

Slates are great for group projects; creativity feeds on creativity, and the more you see how others think, the more ideas you'll have yourself. Best of all, slates are permanent, so it's always there for later. Or, if you don't like what you've written, or you're finished, just wipe the slate clean, and you can start creating more ideas again.

Who are we?

We're a couple of students of web design, free thinking, and inspiration.

Idea Otter started as a personal project of Simon to keep some of his ideas on file. It was also an excuse to make a bookmarklet. Neat. He then told Richard about it, who instantly saw what a great tool it would be if it could just be tweaked like so. We asked Olivia to draw us some otters, and here we are.
